US287

●I rode north out of Augusta after four days with Ted and Maggie. I did so without wearing club colors. Did want anyone associating the club with their house.

●A the bridge over the Sun River, pulled over onto the gravel fishing access road. I got off the bike, and stood looking at the river. Far downriver, I spotted the battered concrete of the old highway bridge, shoved from the alignment a half mile by the great flood of 1964.

●Opened the right saddle bag, the hot one sitting just over the pipes. I pulled my warm Bitterroot Bastards vest out. Threaded my arm through and pulled it onto my back.

●The rest of today’s ride honors Vince. Proud to wear my colors.

●I remounted and started the bike. A quick u-turn spitting gravel, I popped back onto the highway. Ascending the northern canyon side, I let the pipes rip, shifting with precision, leaving only my echo in the empty valley.

●Riding for the next couple hours, detoured through Heart Butte. I approached David’s old dirt road, slowed, then stopped. The brush had grown over it, not quite blocking it, but leaving just about a bike width. I turned and rode up the remaining path.

●The cabin stood largely as David probably left it, but everything overgrown and rank. I parked the bike about where I had parked the Honda that day. I turned the bike to face outward.

●The cabin door still stood open.

●Railroad Creek Ranch

●I turned off US2 on to Railroad Creek Ranch—nervous—couldn’t go straight to my cabin. As I approached the fork, l looked hard at the roaddust—marked—truck tires. I kept moving left. I rode all the way to Gladys’s restaurant and parked next to customer cars.

●Didn’t take the direct path to my cabin—walked the in the gully next to the stream—the water noise would cover any sounds I made. Once I got to the old snag bridge, I crossed over to the other side and connected with my trail. Slowly I crept toward my woodshed and my dust covered Subaru. As my view opened up I recognized Dolph’s truck in front of my porch.

●I gasped and bolted out of the forest into the open. “Dolph! Dolph! Where are you?”

●“Here.” His voice came from behind me, I whipped around. He’d been standing in the woodshed, I had run right by him.

●He didn’t look good—dark circles under his eyes, bruise on the side of his head and a bandage on his left hand.

●I held back from approaching him, thinking about Cherry.

●"Vince is gone."

●My voice hitched. "I know." I started shaking all over again.

●Dolph closed the distance, put his hand on my shoulder.

●Deep breath—hold it back—

●"Vince left us all jobs. Your’s is the last one." He gestured to the Ironhead Sporty on the trailer. "Keep it for Ed. If he wants it, give it back to him, otherwise, it’s yours."
