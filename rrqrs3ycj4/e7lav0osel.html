Goat Lick, Montana

US2 westbound—I stood on the edge of the highway overlooking Goat Lick—a gorge made by the Middle Fork of the Flathead. No riverbanks—just steep talus slopes of oddly white crumbling rock stained with rust red streaks pointing down into the whitewater river.

●I had seen this flash by countless times—today I stopped to marvel at it. Without any sort of proper pullout for gawking, it felt dicey to stand sandwiched between full speed highway traffic and the ribbon of guard rail on a shoulder barely wide enough for the bike.

I remounted the bike—facing west on the eastbound shoulder—and took off, chasing and then pacing the train on the mountainside across the valley.
