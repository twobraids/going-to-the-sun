Lost Trail Hot Springs

●We pulled into the campground just as I started to get hungry. Just a few camp spaces occupied. Chris likely chose the site more for the walking convenience to the lodge than any sense of wanting privacy. We parked the bikes near a thick ponderosa.

●Chris walked off to the office to rent the space. I scoped out a place for my tent, rolled it out, unfolded. The three fiberglass poles, shock corded, snapped themselves together. Sixty seconds later, my tent fully erected, I crawled in checking for level. I had to get out only once, pick it up and move it slightly to . I staked it down.

●"What the fuck is that?" He gesturing wildly to my blue and white ripstop-nylon Sierra Designs Sphinx. "I was gone for two minutes and you've already moved in."

●"It's my backpacking tent. What's wrong with it?"

●"You packed a NASA landing pod?" He examined the poles holding themselves up with tension, the roof tightly suspended beneath them.

●"It's a great tent. Really light, easy to carry for miles."

●"Clever design, so obvious. Goddamn." He pointed at the green canvas roll strapped to his sissy bar. "That ancient thing takes twenty minutes to assemble and will always collapse at least once during the night. Look at the balanced tension on this. It can't fucking fall down."

●He looked at me. "You got room for two in there?"

●I smiled. "Always."

●When Chris and I got into the hot tub, it only took a few minutes for everyone else to leave. The women, specifically, seemed to be really uncomfortable with us—if they only knew the truth.

●After we were alone in the water, I asked, "Can you tell me something about Vince?"

●"Maybe."

●"What's the deal between him and Acid?"

●"Acid? Whaddaya already know?"

●"Vince used to visit him in Deer Lodge. Everybody called him a fuck up." I huffed once. "What I really mean, uh, why does Vince keep calling me Acid?"

●Chris didn't answer for a half a minute. "Vince really fell for Acid back in the day. Years before Vince and I met. They fucked around, but it really wasn't Acid's thing. Acid was Vince's first. He'll always have a soft spot for him."

●He sighed, "I'm sorry that's happening. Yeah, I see the similarities, but, Jesus, he's gotta get over that. I'll talk to him when we get back."

●When we emerged from the canyon onto the broad barren Idaho valley on US93, I recognized the Valley of a Thousand Springs. I immediately scanned the eastern face of the mountains for the fault scarp. Took me a moment to realize the whitish line at the base of the mountains wasn't a road.

●I rolled the throttle, closed the distance with Chris, flipped the highbeam on to catch his attention. When I saw his brake tap acknowledgment, I raised the hand signal to pull over.

●Once stopped, I hopped off the bike and pulled my map from my pack. I kept looking between it and the face of the eastern mountain, Borah Peak.

●Chris just stood there looking at me.

●"We gotta do a detour, I need to see the fault scarp."

●He looked at me with resignation.

●"About two miles ahead there's a dirt road that heads straight for that mountain. Only about two and half miles. I need to see it."

●"What again do you need to see?"

●"The fault scarp. Just a year and a half ago, an earthquake. Six point 9. That mountain right there, Borah Peak, jumped up ten feet higher. This whole valley tilted in seconds. The scarp is the scar. The ground broke. Look!" I pointed to the thin while line demarking the valley from the mountain. "That line. It's not a road."

●Chris grinned at me. "Follow your hard-on."

●"I felt that earthquake all the way up at my cabin on the Rez."
