The Iron Horse

●I stood in the office with Judy, cashing out at the end of my shift. Pretty routine—tips too low—just matter of setting expectations with the card room filled with workers from the aluminum plant. The lumber mill workers tip better. The two groups rarely show up on the same night. Bars have rhythms.

●With cash in hand, I turned to leave and found Dolph and Rooster standing at the door blocking my exit. I stepped toward them expecting they'd part and let me leave. Didn't happen, my boot squeaked on the floor as I stopped.

●"Vince wants to see you," Dolph said.

●Looking at each in turn, I shrugged. "Sure. What's up?" Didn't get an answer.

●The led me directly across the hall where Chris had taken me to the little apartment to fuck. One fluorescent tube the corridor's lights flickered randomly like a weak strobe light. The apartment door sat open.

●Dolph walked right in and Rooster herded me in ahead of him. Vince sat a the table with a pitcher of beer and a what looked like a cigar box. Dolph sat down, reaching for the box. Vince gestured for me to sit. Once my chair, Rooster sat down, too.

●I looked around the table. "Uh, what's up?"

●"I just wanted the three of us to spend some time talking with ya. I've gotten to know ya pretty good, but these guys haven't had the opportunity. Time to change that."

●Over the next half hour, I had an interview. Vince asking me questions about my past, how I got to the Rez, card dealing, and computer programming. Dolph and Rooster didn't really say much.

●Vince smiled at Rooster. "Ask him about Mollies Nipple."

●"Mollies Nipple?" Rooster looked very confused, glanced at me, then back to Vince. "...one of Sarah's?"

●"Jesus Roost', ask him." Vince pointed his muscled arm at me.

●Rooster looked back to me—frowning, irritated.

●"It's gravel. Stretches from US89 to MT358. Starts out arrow straight, but then just rolls over into gully. Curve banked all wrong—it's a rush. The next curve is as perfect as it could be. Goes on for miles, not a moment of riding upright. Every fifth curve is wrong as that first one. Several spots of deep gravel..."

●Vince grinned like he'd just won the pot. Rooster just looked at him with his jaw half open, begging for an explanation.

●I looked back and forth between them, my voice slowing. "It's easy to turn around at the hog farm. Makes it easy to run back through the other way."

●"This is why I put him on Ed's bike." Vince said as if were a logical conclusion.

●"Yeah, I hear ya." Rooster nodded at Vince. Looking back at me, he asked, "You're some kind of accountant?"

●"No, not at all. Programmer." Figured I'd have to take this as simple as I could. "Computers are machines that can follow instructions. I'm good at writing instructions. I make programmed machines that people like Sarah and Chris can use to run businesses."

●Rooster nodded—he didn't get a word of it.

●"Go on. Tell him why you like it." Vince prompted me with his hands.

●I looked warily at him and then my eyes fell on Dolph, madly working on his notepad. Not writing—sketching maybe? My thoughts snagged for a second on him.

●"It's just another type of riding really. You know, on the bike, sometimes the road, the landscape, the engine, the weather, all synchronize perfectly. You're an animal focused on staying alive for the next few seconds, reaching for the next few after that. Best feeling ever. I can tap into that same ecstasy with just my mind while programming."

●"Rooster," Vince leaned forward on the table. "He's like Ed, but with a brain."

●"I don't know what to make of you." Rooster frowned at me with furrowed brow. "You don't fuckin' drink. Scrawny as shit."

●"You said that about Snake Eyes twenty years back." Vince looked smug. "He’s stacked more cash for the Club than all the rest of us put together."

●Rooster response came quick. "Yeah, yeah. But Snake Eyes can strip a bike to the frame and then ride it by sundown."

●Vince locked eyes with Rooster. "Chris’ll apprentice him. Club ain’t just muscle. We need brains, too."

●"Wait a minute," I interrupted. "What does me stripping a bike have to do with anything?"

●Vince looked straight at me, cigar smoldering in his fingers. "Karl, you pledge the Club today. When it’s time for the patch, you’ll own your own bike. You’ll wrench it yourself. That’s how it is. Chris'll train ya up."
