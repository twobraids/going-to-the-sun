Life is performance art.

Motorcycle riding on a twisted mountain road is dancing to the rhythm of a landscape. The road, the static manifestation of that dance: musical notation embodied in asphalt.

Bend, Oregon 2009
About my writing in the past:
A Road Not Ridden

A book awaits a reader. Software awaits a thread of execution. A road awaits a rider.

I've always had a difficult time calling myself a writer. It seems presumptuous for me to do so. However, I have no hesitation in calling myself a programmer.

Think of this as a program. I'm writing the instructions for placing specific images and emotions into the mind of the reader. The reader dances to words that I've written on the road.

This is a work of fiction based on events and places in my life.

I'm not writing chapters in chronological order. I'm jumping around. Juggling continuity while trying to keep my grip on the handlebars.

What's to become of this writing? I'm not wasting my time looking for a publisher—that industry, a shadow of its past, is now only for the extraordinary. Self-publishing through a service is just a money-harvesting scam.

I'm posting here only for those I invite to share this mad ride.

Eventually, these words will vanish. That is always the nature of performance art.

Rough drafts update about every six hours.

Yes, I know that 60% of this will be brutally cut if this project ever goes beyond the rough draft stage.
